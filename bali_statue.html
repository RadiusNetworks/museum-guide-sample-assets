Hanuman Statue

Bali, Indonesia 2003

Sandstone

Balinese stone carvers carry on the rich tradition of Hindu arts in what is now a predominantly Muslim nation. Today, most stone carvers focus their energies on export-oriented subjects like dolphins or Buddha figures, but it is in traditional themes like this Hindu monkey god that Balinese craftsmanship really shines. Note the rich detail including spiral eyes, textured skullcap, and jeweled necklace. This piece would have been one of two placed atop adjacent pedestals to form a gateway.
